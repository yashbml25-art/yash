Ada Lovelace

The World's First Computer Programmer

Ada Lovelace in her early years, envisioning the "poetical science."

The Visionary

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, was an English mathematician and writer. She is chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Life Timeline

  • 1815: Born in London to Lord Byron and Anne Isabella Milbanke.
  • 1833: Meets Charles Babbage, the "father of the computer."
  • 1842: Translates Luigi Menabrea's memoir on the Analytical Engine, adding her own extensive "Notes."
  • 1843: Her notes describe an algorithm for the Engine to calculate Bernoulli numbers, now considered the first computer program.

Her Legacy

"The Analytical Engine weaves algebraic patterns just as the Jacquard loom weaves flowers and leaves."

Ada's unique perspective—which she called "poetical science"—led her to realize that computers could do more than just crunch numbers; they could create music, art, and complex graphics.
